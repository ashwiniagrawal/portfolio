Case study — Restaurant operations SaaS

General Manager Virtual Agent

An internal AI assistant and operations dashboard that lets restaurant GMs run a shift without switching between a POS, a scheduling tool, and a group text — built to answer a question or show the numbers, whichever the moment calls for.

Role
Product & UI Designer
Platform
Tablet-first web app
Stack
React, Tailwind, Zustand
Industry
Hospitality

Overview

The problem

A restaurant GM's shift is interruption-driven — a stockout, a slow ticket, a guest complaint, all arriving at once. The tools meant to help were built for someone sitting at a desk: a POS for orders, a separate scheduling tool, and a group text for anything that didn't fit either. By the time a problem showed up in a report, the guest had already noticed.

The brief was to design a single surface a GM could glance at mid-shift or ask a direct question, without learning a new system or digging through menus while holding a tray.

User persona

Who this was designed for

JE

Jordan Ellis

General Manager · 15-unit casual dining group · on the floor 90% of shift

"I don't need another dashboard to babysit. I need to know what's wrong before a guest tells me."

Goals
  • Keep the floor running without micromanaging every ticket
  • Catch stockouts and slow orders before they become complaints
  • Get a straight answer without hunting through three systems
Frustrations
  • Switches between a POS, scheduling tool, and group text all shift
  • Problems only show up in reports after service is already affected
  • No way to just ask a system a question — everything is menus

Requirements

What the design had to do

01

Surface stockouts and slow-SLA orders on their own, without a GM having to pull a report.

02

Let a GM ask a plain-language question and get a direct answer, alongside the structured data.

03

Keep high-frequency actions — 86 an item, override a price — one tap away, not nested in menus.

04

Hold up on a shared tablet at the pass, used one-handed, in a kitchen environment.

Process

How we got there

Discover

Shadow real shifts

Observed GMs during dinner service to see exactly where the existing tools broke down in the moment.

Define

Split status from inquiry

Mapped which moments needed a glance at status versus a specific question answered — and designed around that line.

Design

Two panes, one system

Prototyped a dual-pane layout: structured data on one side, a conversational assistant on the other.

Deliver

Build for reuse

Shipped a React + Zustand component library so new flows — Guest Recovery, SMS Emulator — didn't require redesigning the shell.

Wireframes

Early structure

Low-fidelity passes used to agree on layout and hierarchy before any visual design — where status lives, where the assistant lives, and what stays one tap away.

Top bar & guest menu availability
  • 1Context cards swap the whole right panel — dashed border marks the active flow so a GM always knows what they're looking at.
  • 2Search and notifications stay in the top bar across every flow, so the shell never has to be relearned.
  • 3Status pill is the one thing a GM scans for — color plus label, never color alone, for anyone colorblind on the floor.
Order queue as a scan-first table
  • 1SLA breach shifts the whole row's fill, not just the time — visible in peripheral vision from across the pass.
  • 2Quick chips turn the assistant's most common asks into one tap, so the chat pane is an option, not a requirement.
  • 3Two running totals stay visible at all times — the numbers a GM reports on at end of shift, not buried in a report.
Conversational thread beside the data
  • 1Pane width is adjustable, not fixed — a GM can widen it during a slow stretch or shrink it back during rush.
  • 2Assistant replies can run long (a full answer), but never push the input or the KDS table off screen.
  • 3A visible typing state matters here — silence during a busy shift reads as the system being broken, not thinking.

Final design

Where it landed

The shipped interface pairs the flow-context cards and Mini-KDS from the wireframes with a live chat pane, so a GM can act on what they see or ask the assistant directly — same screen, same shift.

Outcome

What changed

Status, stock, and SLA readable in one glance mid-shift, without opening a report Chat and dashboard treated as one system, so a question never requires leaving the screen A reusable component shell that let later features (Guest Recovery, SMS Emulator) ship without a redesign Used in leadership demos and internal showcases as a reference for the assistant-plus-dashboard pattern
